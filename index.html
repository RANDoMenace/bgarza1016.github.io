About Me

I am a recent graduate from General Assembly in Downtown Los Angeles. Each three weeks we would learn the basics of a language and turning that information into a restful application. I've studied HTML, CSS, Javascript, Ruby on Rails, and the MEAN stack. There we worked on projects using GitHub for tracking our progress, Trello for agile development, and Black/ White Hat Scrum for our retrospectives. I am used to working solo, but have experience also working in a group project or two.

I can honestly say since I was young I was very interested in programming. Although it was not my first career choice I gained a good sense for teamwork and communication from my days as a firefighter. In the end I was in search for a place where I belonged, and ended up leaving the fire service.

My next choice was Animation, because I was somewhat of an artist in my spare time. After three years of schooling I still found myself gravitating towards scripting.

Still in search for where I belonged I moved into information technology with the help of some good friends. I am a curious self-starter enthusiastic about self-teaching and problem solving.

Now with the arrival of my baby girl Abigale, I finally decided to go BIG or stay in IT. I decided to attend General Assembly's Web Development Immersive course. I couldn't be happier about this decision. I love the people and the work I do. I now know who I am, I am a programmer.
